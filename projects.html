Internship Project

During my internship at Verizon, I developed a predictive tool for the network team to help assess project acceleration or deceleration based on the anticipated remaining commit and spend. Using data from their project management tool, I began by performing data preprocessing and creating visualizations to better understand the key factors influencing a project's commit and spend.
Next, I trained and tested a Random Forest regression model to predict commit and spend based on specific project factors. To enhance the model’s accuracy, I experimented with various combinations of input features, fine-tuning the model’s performance. After validation, I deployed the model to calculate commit and spend for unseen data.
This tool empowers the network team to make more informed decisions regarding budget allocation and project timelines, improving overall project management efficiency.

Field Session Project

This project was completed as part of a field session, a capstone course at my university. For this project, I collaborated with a team of three to assist our client, the Rocky Mountain Lab Rescue—a nonprofit organization dedicated to rescuing and rehoming dogs. The rescue relies heavily on donations generated from merchandise sales, both online and at in-person events. Our client wanted to make more informed decisions when ordering new merchandise by analyzing inventory trends. They requested that our team build a dashboard to visualize their inventory data and predict future inventory needs based on key factors.
To tackle this project, our team divided the work into three components: front-end development, back-end development, and machine learning. I was responsible for the machine learning component. My role involved performing data preprocessing and exploratory data analysis to prepare the data for modeling. I then developed a Random Forest regression model to predict future sales based on factors such as the time of year and the type of merchandise.
The final deliverable was a dashboard that displays the current inventory alongside predicted inventory levels for the next year, categorized by item. While the model was trained on just four months of data, its accuracy will improve as more data is added.
Our client was very pleased with the dashboard, as it will enable them to better anticipate periods of high demand—especially during specific seasons and after major events.

Assessing the Predictive Power of Tumor Smoothness and Concavity in Breast Cancer Diagnosis Using Generalized Linear Models

In this project, I aimed to answer the question:
Can the characteristics of smoothness and concavity effectively predict whether a breast cancer diagnosis is malignant (‘M’) or benign (‘B’)?
Using the breast cancer data set from the UC Irvine’s Machine Learning Repository, I explored the relationship between tumor smoothness and concavity and breast cancer diagnosis. I fitted a generalized linear model to predict the diagnosis based on these two features. I evaluated the models’ performance and interpreted the results, connecting back to the original question.
